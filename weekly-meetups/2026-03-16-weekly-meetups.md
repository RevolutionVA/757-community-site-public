#### Brian Knox: Stop Rewriting DAX: User-Defined Functions & the DAX Lib Ecosystem

- **Time:** 06:00 PM
- **Group:** Hampton Roads SQL Server User Group
- **Link:** [Event Link](https://www.meetup.com/hampton-roads-sql-server-user-group/events/313526061/)

**Description:**
**IN PERSON MEETING!!!!!**
For years, DAX developers have lived with a dirty secret: copy-paste is our version control. Complex measures get duplicated across models, tweaked slightly, and inevitably drift out of sync. Calculation groups helped, but they came with their own complexity and limitations. This session takes you from zero to productive with User-Defined Functions. Introduced in September 2025, UDFs let you write reusable, parameterized functions in DAX for the first time and DAX Lib (daxlib.org) gives you 30+ community-built function packages to import and use immediately. This session walks through UDF fundamentals, live imports from DAX Lib, and practical examples of replacing duplicated measure logic with clean, maintainable function calls.

#### 10 Years A Kotlin Dev

- **Time:** 06:30 PM
- **Group:** Hampton Roads Java Users Group
- **Link:** [Event Link](https://www.meetup.com/hampton-roads-java-users-group/events/312633477/)

**Description:**
Kotlin has emerged as a compelling choice for both Android and backend development, offering a modern language with strong null-safety, concise syntax, and excellent interoperability with Java. In this meetup, we'll explore the key advantages that drive teams to adopt Kotlin—from improved developer productivity and safer Android apps to structured concurrency with coroutines on the backend. We'll highlight how Kotlin aligns with today's Android ecosystem, integrates with popular backend frameworks, and enables cleaner, more maintainable code across platforms. At the same time, adopting Kotlin is not without challenges. We'll take an honest look at the tradeoffs, including learning curve considerations, build performance, interoperability friction with legacy Java code, and operational concerns in backend systems. Through a retrospective lens, this talk will share practical lessons learned and guidance on when Kotlin is the right choice—and when it may not be—helping attendees make informed decisions for their Android and server-side projects.

#### 🧜‍♀️ AICHR | Guardrails for Gen AI: From Policy to Practice

- **Time:** 06:30 PM
- **Group:** 757 Artificial Intelligence Collective
- **Link:** [Event Link](https://www.meetup.com/aicollectivehr/events/313055669/)

**Description:**
Guardrails for Gen AI: From Policy to Practice
How to constrain AI systems without killing their value

Everyone uses the word "guardrails." Almost nobody means the same thing.

This co-presented session brings together AI strategy and cybersecurity expertise to break down what guardrails actually are, where they belong in your AI systems, and how to implement them without crippling the tools your teams rely on. We will cover the strategic layer — governance, risk classification, and authority boundaries — then dive into the technical layer with hands-on walkthroughs of real guardrails frameworks like NVIDIA NeMo Guardrails and Guardrails AI. Whether you are a business leader trying to deploy AI safely or a security practitioner trying to contain it, this session gives you a practical, layered approach you can take back to your organization immediately.

**What you'll learn**

* Why traditional security assumptions break down with AI systems
* The three layers of AI guardrails: governance, architecture, and implementation
* How to treat AI agents as high-risk identities using least privilege and Zero Trust principles
* Where guardrails actually get enforced — at system boundaries, not in prompts
* Real tools and frameworks for building guardrails into AI pipelines
* What guardrails can and cannot do — honest limitations and tradeoffs
* Hands-on homework: get started with Guardrails AI and NeMo Guardrails in 15 minutes

This is not about adding a system prompt and hoping for the best. It is about building AI systems that fail safely and stay auditable.

**Your speakers**

Andrew Stafford is a Fractional Chief AI Officer and Director of Technology and Global Alliances at The AI Collective. He works with executive teams to establish clear ownership of AI decisions, focus on real ROI, and avoid unnecessary risk, tool sprawl, and hype-driven strategy. Andrew brings 25 years in tech spanning Amazon, NASA, and five startups to the strategic and architectural side of AI security.

Ken is a security practitioner with ISSA Hampton Roads. He brings deep hands-on experience in security engineering and implementation, and will lead the technical deep dive into guardrails tooling, configuration, and real-world limitations.
Together, Andrew and Ken will walk you through a complete guardrails framework — from policy to architecture to working code — blending strategic thinking with practical implementation.

**Who should attend?**

* CISOs, security architects, and IT leaders responsible for AI risk
* Developers and engineers building or deploying AI agents and applications
* Product managers and business leaders evaluating AI tools for their teams
* Security practitioners curious about the AI threat model
* Anyone responsible for AI governance, compliance, or acceptable use policies
* Technical professionals who want hands-on experience with guardrails tools

No prior AI security experience required, but familiarity with basic AI concepts is helpful.

**Why this matters**

AI systems collapse data, logic, and control — and traditional security was never built for that. The real danger is not bad AI. It is trusting non-deterministic systems with authority. Organizations that get guardrails right will deploy AI faster and safer. Organizations that skip them will learn the hard way. This session is about making sure you are in the first group.
